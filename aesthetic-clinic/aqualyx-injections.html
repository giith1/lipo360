Understanding Stress Incontinence

Stress incontinence is a prevalent form of urinary incontinence that affects women across all age groups. It occurs when physical activities—like sneezing, coughing, laughing, or exercising—put pressure on the bladder, causing unintentional urine leakage.

This condition can significantly impact quality of life, leading many women to avoid social interactions out of embarrassment. However, effective treatments are available to alleviate these symptoms and help you regain control.

What Causes Stress Incontinence?

Stress incontinence happens when activities that increase abdominal pressure, such as coughing, sneezing, laughing, exercising, or lifting heavy objects, lead to bladder pressure that exceeds urethral resistance. While the condition can affect both men and women, it is more common in women—particularly those who have given birth or experienced menopause.

Aqualyx Fat Dissolving Injections - FAQS
1. What is Aqualyx, and how does it work?

Aqualyx is a non-surgical fat-dissolving injection designed to target and eliminate stubborn fat deposits. It contains deoxycholic acid, which breaks down fat cells. Once injected, the solution liquefies fat cells, which are then naturally eliminated by the body through the lymphatic system. Think of it as a "magic eraser" for unwanted fat—but only in small, localized areas.

2. Which areas of the body can be treated with Aqualyx?

Aqualyx is effective for treating areas with stubborn fat that resist diet and exercise. Common treatment areas include:

  • Double chin

  • Upper arms

  • Abdomen and love handles

  • Inner and outer thighs

  • Back (bra bulge)

  • Hips (saddlebags)

  • Knees

So, if you’ve been battling a “dad bod” belly or a persistent double chin, Aqualyx might be your new best friend.

3. Is Aqualyx a weight-loss treatment?

No, Aqualyx is not a weight-loss solution. It’s a body contouring treatment designed for people who are close to their ideal weight but struggle with localized fat pockets. If you’re expecting to shed 20 pounds overnight, this isn’t the answer—but it will help sculpt and refine your shape.

4. How many treatments are needed to see results?

Results vary, but most people require 2 to 5 sessions, spaced about 4 to 6 weeks apart, for optimal results. Some notice improvement after the first session, but full results typically take a few months as the body processes and eliminates the fat. Patience is key—this isn’t an overnight miracle, but rather a gradual transformation.

5. Are there any side effects or downtime?

Like any cosmetic procedure, Aqualyx has some temporary side effects. You may experience:

  • Swelling and redness

  • Bruising

  • Tenderness or mild discomfort

  • Itchiness

Most side effects subside within a few days, but some swelling can last up to two weeks (so maybe don’t schedule your treatment right before a big event). There’s no major downtime, but avoiding alcohol, strenuous exercise, and saunas for a few days is recommended.
