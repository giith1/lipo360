What Are Solar Lentigines?

Often mistaken for freckles, solar lentigines are usually flat or slightly raised brown lesions with a well-defined edge. They can vary in color, ranging from tan to dark brown. Unlike freckles, which are typically uniform in color, solar lentigines are irregular and may appear on sun-exposed areas such as your face, hands, or lower legs. Sometimes, they can even appear following a sunburn.

Despite their brownish hue, these spots are not linked to liver disease, as they’re sometimes mistakenly called "liver spots." Instead, they’re simply a visible sign of how your skin has responded to sun damage over time. For those seeking to remove these age spots, treatments like CryoPen for Solar Lentigines Removal have proven to be effective. This method offers a precise and efficient way to target and eliminate those pesky marks without causing damage to the surrounding skin.

CryoPen Treatment: Freeze Away Sun Spots

CryoPen is a form of cryotherapy that utilizes a fine jet of nitrous oxide to freeze and destroy the abnormal tissue. This device delivers a jet of cold gas under high pressure, allowing for precise treatment of solar lentigines with minimal discomfort. The treatment is quick, effective, and typically only takes a few seconds per spot.
