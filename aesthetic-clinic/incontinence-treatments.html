Understanding Stress Incontinence

Stress incontinence is a prevalent form of urinary incontinence that affects women across all age groups. It occurs when physical activities—like sneezing, coughing, laughing, or exercising—put pressure on the bladder, causing unintentional urine leakage.

This condition can significantly impact quality of life, leading many women to avoid social interactions out of embarrassment. However, effective treatments are available to alleviate these symptoms and help you regain control.

What Causes Stress Incontinence?

Stress incontinence happens when activities that increase abdominal pressure, such as coughing, sneezing, laughing, exercising, or lifting heavy objects, lead to bladder pressure that exceeds urethral resistance. While the condition can affect both men and women, it is more common in women—particularly those who have given birth or experienced menopause.
